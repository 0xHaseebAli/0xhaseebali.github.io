Incident report analysis  
Summary  
On 04-01-2026, the organization experienced a Distributed Denial of  
Service (DDoS) attack that disrupted internal network services for  
approximately two hours. The attack involved a flood of ICMP packets that  
overwhelmed the network, preventing normal traffic flow. The incident  
response team quickly mitigated the issue, restoring critical services and  
initiating an investigation to identify the source of the traffic.  
Identify  
During the initial assessment, the cybersecurity team discovered that  
a critical network firewall had been left unconfigured, exposing the  
internal network to external traffic. This misconfiguration created a  
vulnerability that allowed a threat actor to launch an ICMP flood  
attack. The oversight in firewall configuration highlighted a gap in the  
organization's asset management and network hardening policies.  
Protect  
To prevent future attacks, the team implemented several protective  
measures. A new firewall rule was configured to limit the rate of incoming  
ICMP packets. Source IP verification was enabled to detect and block  
spoofed addresses. Additionally, network monitoring software was deployed  
to identify abnormal traffic patterns, and an IDS/IPS system was installed to  
filter ICMP traffic based on suspicious behavior.  
Detect  
The team has configured the firewall and IDS/IPS systems to monitor and  
alert on abnormal ICMP traffic patterns, such as high-frequency ping  
requests or spoofed IP addresses. Detection capabilities now include  
real-time traffic analysis and logging to identify potential DDoS activity  
before it disrupts services.  
Respond  
During the attack, the incident management team acted quickly by blocking  
incoming ICMP traffic at the firewall and shutting down all non-critical network  
services to reduce load on the infrastructure. Critical services were prioritized  
and restored to maintain essential operations. The cybersecurity team  
conducted a rapid investigation, confirming that the attack originated from a  
misconfigured firewall, and worked closely with the network team to isolate  
the source and prevent further disruption.  
Recover  
After the attack, all non-critical services were gradually restored. The team  
conducted a post-incident review and applied necessary updates to  
strengthen resilience against similar future attacks. All users were notified  
once services had returned to normal.
